Inpatient Outcomes of Adrenal Insufficiency in Patients Hospitalized for Sepsis: A Retrospective Analysis of the Nationwide Inpatient Sample (NIS), 2017-2022

Analysis for NIS IM-6

Author
Affiliation

Dua Ali

Dow University of Health Sciences

Preamble:

  • Reference Papers:

  • Lyu et al. 2024

  • Salman et al. 2025

  • Study Objective: To compare in-hospital outcomes and complications between sepsis patients with and without adrenal insufficiency in U.S. hospitals, adjusting for demographic, socioeconomic, clinical, and hospital-related factors.

  • Data Source: Cross-sectional analysis using the National Inpatient Sample (NIS) database, a representative sample of all-payer inpatient hospitalizations in the U.S., from 2017 to 2022.

  • Patient Selection: Included all inpatient admissions for patients 18 years or older with a primary diagnosis of sepsis, identified using ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes (A021, A207, A227, A267, A327, A392–A394, A40x, A41x, A427, A5486, B377, R6520–R6521). From this cohort, patients were categorized into two groups based on the presence or absence of a secondary diagnosis of adrenal insufficiency, identified using ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes (A391, E271–E273, E2740, E2749, E896).

  • Outcomes of Interest:

    • In-hospital Mortality

    • Septic Shock

    • Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

    • Length of Stay (days)

    • Inflation-adjusted Total Charge ($) [adjusted to 2022 dollars, using CPI data by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics]

  • Statistical Analysis: Univariable and multivariable analyses were conducted to evaluate the association between adrenal insufficiency and inpatient outcomes among sepsis hospitalizations:

    • Univariable Analysis:

    • Categorical variables: Pearson’s X² test with Rao & Scott adjustment to account for survey design.

      • Continuous variables: Design-based Kruskal-Wallis test, a non-parametric alternative suitable for survey-weighted data

    • Multivariable Analysis:

    • Binary outcomes (in-hospital mortality, discharge disposition, prolonged mechanical ventilation, pneumonia): Survey-weighted logistic regression using the quasibinomial family.

    • Continuous outcomes (length of stay): Survey-weighted linear regression using the gaussian family.

    • Results reported as adjusted odds ratios (aORs) or beta coefficients with 95% confidence intervals

    • Adjustments: Models controlled for the following covariates:

      • Demographics and Socioeconomic Factors: Age, Sex, Race, Residential income, Expected primary payer.

      • Clinical Factors: Diabetes Mellitus, Chronic Kidney Disease, Liver Disease, Heart Failure, Obesity, Hypertension, Hyperlipidemia, Smoking,

      • Hospital-Level Factors: Hospital region, Hospital bedsize, Hospital location and teaching status.

  • Software: All analyses were performed using R Statistical Language (Version 4.5.0; R Foundation for Statistical Computing, Vienna, Austria), incorporating survey-weighted procedures via the survey package to account for the complex sampling design of NIS.
